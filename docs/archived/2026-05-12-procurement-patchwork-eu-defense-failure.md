# The Procurement Patchwork: Why Europe's Defense Ambition is Failing its Own Test

**The European Union has set a bold target: by 2030, 40% of its defense materiel should be procured collaboratively. On paper, the tools are there—EDIRPA, EDIP, and the Defence Readiness Roadmap. But in practice, Europe is not building a shield; it is assembling a patchwork. As national capitals continue to prioritize their own industrial champions over collective security, the EU's quest for strategic autonomy is being strangled by the very procurement processes meant to enable it.**

## The Illusion of Cooperation

For decades, European defense has been characterized by "national champions." Every medium-to-large state wants its own fighter jet, its own tank, and its own missile system. While this protects domestic jobs and industrial bases, it creates a logistical nightmare: a fragmented fleet of equipment that cannot communicate, cannot be maintained by a single supply chain, and cannot be deployed rapidly in a unified manner.

The EU's current strategy is to *incentivize* joint procurement through grants and loans. The logic is simple: if the EU pays for part of the project, Member States will be more likely to cooperate. 

The problem is that incentives are not the same as mandates. When a grant from the European Defence Industry Programme (EDIP) is weighed against the political pressure to maintain a specific factory in a specific electoral district, the grant often loses. The result is "joint procurement" in name only—projects that are nominally collaborative but remain bogged down by national requirements and industrial disputes.

## The Innovation Gap

This fragmentation is not just a logistical failure; it is a technological one. While the US leverages a massive, unified Department of Defense (DoD) to scale innovations from startups to the battlefield, Europe remains a collection of siloed bureaucracies.

As noted by Bruegel, the number of defense startups in the US dwarfs those in Europe. Why? Because a startup in the US has one primary customer with a clear set of requirements and a massive budget. A startup in Europe faces 27 different procurement regimes, 27 different sets of certifications, and 27 different political whims.

By the time a European "joint" project reaches the procurement stage, the technology is often already obsolete, outpaced by the agility of a unified market.

## Analysis: From Incentives to Agency

Patria's view: Europe cannot "incentivize" its way to strategic autonomy. You cannot build a sovereign defense capability using a system of voluntary grants and "capability coalitions." 

If the EU is serious about its 2030 targets, it must move beyond the role of a financier and become a central authority. We need a **European Defense Procurement Agency (EDPA)** with teeth.

The EDPA should not just manage grants; it should have the mandate to:
1. **Enforce Interoperability**: Mandate that any equipment procured with EU funds must meet strict, non-negotiable interoperability standards.
2. **Penalize Unilateralism**: Implement a "strategic tax" or a reduction in other EU funds for Member States that unilaterally procure duplicate capabilities that undermine the collective effort.
3. **Direct Innovation**: Act as a single "Buyer of First Resort" for European defense startups, providing a clear path from prototype to scale across the entire Union.

Strategic autonomy is not a product of financial aid; it is a product of political will and institutional power. As long as procurement is treated as a national hobby, Europe will remain a security consumer rather than a security provider.

## Conclusion

The 40% joint procurement target is a necessary ambition, but without a centralized agency to enforce it, it is a hollow number. Europe is currently treating defense as a series of transactions; it needs to treat it as a sovereign function.

The choice is clear: either Europe builds a unified procurement architecture that can compete with the US and China, or it continues to buy a fragmented collection of tools that it cannot actually use together. The patchwork is no longer an option.
