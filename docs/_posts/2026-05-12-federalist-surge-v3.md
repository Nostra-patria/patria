**On May 9, 2026, the soft-launch of Astra Europa replaced the cautious rhetoric of 'European integration' with a direct demand for a federal state capable of competing with global superpowers.**

## The Forwardist Front

The movement, described as "liberal, federalist, and forwardist," emerged on Europe Day as a consolidation of the intellectual federalist wing. Led by a coalition of former Ave Europa leaders—including Rolf Strijdhorst and Nikodem Skrobisz—alongside policy experts like Giorgio Cacciaguerra Ranghieri, Astra Europa rejects the "tribal identitarianism" and "green degrowth" that have stalled the Union's momentum. Their program uses 12 "Guiding Stars" to move the EU beyond the reactive, intergovernmental deadlock of the Council.

The reception was immediate. Within 24 hours, the French daily *La Croix* reported on the launch, and the movement recorded thousands of newsletter sign-ups. According to Nikodem Skrobisz, the resonance of the launch exceeded internal expectations, indicating a growing appetite among European professional and creative classes for a "sovereignty through scale" approach. Patria's view: this is a strategic pivot toward a European state that prioritizes scientific progress and technological optimism over the fragmented bureaucracy of 27 member states.

## The Mechanics of a Federalist Surge

The current momentum toward a European Federation is a pragmatic reaction to the failure of the intergovernmental method. For decades, the "Conference Method"—where heads of state negotiate in closed rooms—has prioritized the lowest common denominator. This has left the EU paralyzed during systemic crises. The shift toward a federal structure is a direct demand for efficiency and democratic legitimacy.

The catalyst is the realization that sovereignty is not a static asset to be guarded, but a capacity to be exercised. When member states act individually, they are price-takers in a global economy dominated by the US and China. By pooling sovereignty, Europe transforms from a collection of fragmented markets into a single, coherent geopolitical actor. This is the essence of "European Strategic Autonomy": the ability to act decisively without external permission.

## The Astra Europa Influence and the 12 Stars

The emergence of Astra Europa represents an evolution in the federalist project. Unlike the technocratic integration of the past, driven by "spill-over" effects and administrative necessity, the new surge is explicitly political. The 12 Guiding Stars framework bridges high-level constitutional theory and concrete policy goals.

By framing federation through "Ambition & Discovery" (Star 11) and "Prosperity Through Scale" (Star 8), these movements decouple federalism from the fear of "Brussels bureaucracy." They present it as a tool for empowerment. This shifts the focus from the loss of national identity to the gain of collective agency.

## Overcoming the Sovereignty Trap

The primary obstacle remains the "Sovereignty Trap"—the belief that any transfer of power to a central authority is a zero-sum loss. Recent geopolitical data suggests the opposite. In energy sovereignty (Star 5) and joint defense procurement (Star 2), the cost of fragmentation now exceeds the cost of integration.

Transitioning to a federal state requires a shift in the European psyche: moving from a "union of states" to a "union of citizens." This requires institutional reform—such as the abolition of the unanimity rule in the Council—and a cultural shift toward a shared European identity (Star 12) that complements national belonging.

## Strategic Imperatives for a Federal Europe

To move beyond the current impasse, the European project must prioritize three systemic shifts:

1. **Democratic Accountability**: Shifting power from the Council to the European Parliament to ensure federalism is a democratic expansion, not a "deficit."
2. **Fiscal Capacity**: Establishing a permanent federal budget for strategic investments in energy and technology, replacing the temporary nature of the Recovery and Resilience Facility.
3. **Unified External Action**: Transitioning from coordinated national foreign policies to a single, coherent European Foreign Policy.

The surge we are witnessing is the first time in a generation that the federalist dream is articulated as an urgent strategic necessity. The gap between the current state of the Union and the requirements of a global superpower is where the opportunity for a federalist breakthrough lies.

The rise of Astra Europa and Volt signals a shift: citizens are no longer asking *if* Europe should integrate further, but *how* it can do so while remaining democratically accountable. For a federation to be legitimate, it must be a project of citizens. The 12 Guiding Stars provide the framework; the political will must come from a bottom-up demand for sovereignty as a collective capacity to act.

Europe stands at a crossroads. It can manage its decline through cautious intergovernmentalism or embrace federation to secure its future. The choice is no longer between the nation-state and the Union, but between a dysfunctional mosaic and a sovereign federation. The question is whether the institutions can keep pace with the ambition.
