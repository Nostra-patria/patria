---
headline: EU-Mercosur Trade Deal Enters Provisional Application
excerpt: The EU-Mercosur Interim Trade Agreement began provisional application on May 1, 2026, reducing tariffs on European exports.
---

The EU-Mercosur Interim Trade Agreement began provisional application on May 1, 2026, following an announcement by European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen. The deal reduces tariffs on European exports, including cars and pharmaceuticals, to secure a strategic trade bloc in a fragmented global market.

The Interim Trade Agreement (iTA) immediately reduces or eliminates tariffs on key European exports, with a particular focus on pharmaceuticals and the automotive sector. This provisional application allows the trade bloc to function while the broader Partnership Agreement awaits full ratification.

The deal follows a period of intense internal friction. In January 2026, French farmers staged major protests in Paris, arguing that the agreement threatens local agriculture by creating unfair competition. Despite these protests and a request from Italy for additional review time, EU Member States agreed to support the signing of the agreement on January 9, 2026.

The provisional status applies to Mercosur countries that completed their own ratification processes by March 2026, including Argentina, Brazil, and Uruguay. The Commission has framed the deal as a strategic necessity to secure a massive trade bloc and maintain competitiveness in a fragmented global market.

The agreement now moves toward full ratification of the broader Partnership Agreement. This final stage will require formal approval from the European Parliament and the national parliaments of the EU Member States and Mercosur partners to permanently solidify the trade and political ties.
